Abstract

Language Models (LMs) represent complex systems that are difficult to manage and deploy safely. For this reason, various techniques have been proposed over time with the aim of detoxifying and controlling the behaviour of the models after their training process. To this end, this work focuses on exploring the most popular fine-tuning and Reinforcement Learning techniques, analysing not only which technique is best for the purpose of detoxification but also how the output of LMs is affected by this choice.
